On the word

Not genius as in good at something.

The word is older and deeper than the way we tend to use it. To the Romans, your genius was the guardian spirit present at your birth — kin to the daimon the Greeks spoke of, the one Socrates said whispered to him at the crossroads of a choice.

It shares a root with genesis and generate: the genius is the generative spark in you — the part that brings something into being that only you could bring. Not a talent you perform. Not a score you're ranked by. The essence you were seeded with, and are here, across a whole lifetime, to unfold.

Everyone is born with one. Most people never quite meet theirs. Human Design and the Gene Keys are two maps to the same territory — the coordinates of your particular genius, written in the sky the moment you arrived. This is a journey into learning to read them.

The first movement

It all begins within.

The mission never changes: to keep discovering your genius, and to enjoy its fruits. And every fruit begins in the same soil — your own transformation. Not the world first. You first.

"Yesterday I was clever, so I wanted to change the world. Today I am wise, so I am changing myself."

— attributed to Rumi

The door to this only opens from the inside — Rumi knew it, and so does your own body. So that's where we start: within. Quietly, honestly, and without rushing.

Because the inner change doesn't stay inner. As it takes root, the real joy begins — watching, with curiosity and anticipation, where it lands out in your life. The work radiates.

The work
Y(our) journey

Most readings hand you a verdict — here's who you are, decoded and done. This is the opposite of that.

Your chart is the loom. The weaving happens between us. I read you partly through my own design — because that's the only honest way one person ever truly meets another — which makes this a dyad: a shared, co-evolving field, where the insight arrives in the space between us.

And it's an adventure — alive, unhurried, endlessly curious — the most interesting one there is, because the terrain we're exploring is you. You bring your genius. I bring the questions. And the rest is inquiry, veneration, celebration, and truth.

How it deepens

A pearl is made in layers.

Nacre laid over grit, one luminous coat at a time. Nothing true about you resolves in a single sitting — so your reading is never finished. It's returned to, refined, deepened, each conversation adding a luster the last one couldn't reach.

I

The first reading

We begin with your chart — your type, your authority, the shape of you. A living document, written to be lived with, not filed away.

II

The contemplation

We talk. The work is deeply contemplative — less about the mechanics than about you, and the questions the chart opens. This is where the field does its quiet work.

III

The refinement loop

What surfaces feeds back into the reading. It grows more accurate the longer we go — because I'm not describing a stranger anymore. I'm reflecting someone I've come to see.

IV

The community

In time, a small circle — where charts meet charts, not to compare in competition but to learn by reflection. The oldest way to do this work, and still the most powerful: seen by others who are being seen.

Between the calls

A companion, any hour.

For those who want it, a private guide on WhatsApp — grounded in your chart and this work — there whenever you are. A question at 2am. A pattern you just caught. A choice sitting in front of you tonight. It meets you in the moment, so the work doesn't pause when the call ends.

It isn't a replacement for our conversations; it's the thread that keeps them alive between them. And I'm present in that field too — dropping in when it helps, witnessing, adding a word. You're never in there alone.

The companion is honest about what it is: it holds the mechanics and keeps good questions coming. The depth — the seeing, the alchemy — is what the two of us do together. Available on its own, or woven into the journeys below.

The daily weather

The sky is always moving.

Each day the planets pass into new gates — a kind of weather moving through the field that everyone can feel. If you'd like it, your companion sends you the day's, or the week's, weather: what's in the air for all of us, and — once your chart is in the field — how today meets you in particular. It isn't fortune-telling. It's a small daily invitation to presence — what am I responding to today? — that keeps you close to your own design.
